Jumps

Toe Loop

The skater starts this movement going backwards on the outside edge of the right skate. With the assistance of the left toe pick hitting the ice, the skater takes off and after rotation in the air, lands on the outside edge of the right skate. This is considered the easiest jump.

Flip

This jump is similar to Toe Loop but starts on the opposite foot. A right handed skater takes off from the back inside edge of the left skate with an assist from the right toe pick, and lands on the back outside edge of the right skate.

Lutz

In a Lutz, the skater also digs their right toe pick hitting the ice, but unlike a Too Loop or a Flip the left foot skates on the back outside edge. This means that the skater takes off and lands on same edge and opposite foot, with a counter-rotation in the air.

Salchow

Without assistance from the free foot, the skater starts the Salchow from the back inside edge, swings the free leg forward, completes the turn in the air and lands on the back outside edge of the opposite foot. If the skater is right-handed, the jump would start on the left leg and land on the right.

Loop

This jump the same as a Toe Loop but without the assistance of the toe pick in take-off. The skater takes off from the back outside edge of the right foot, then swivels arms to the right, moves the left leg forward and lands on the back outside edge of the right foot (takes off and lands on the same edge and foot).

Axel

The Axel is the only jump in which the skater takes off facing forward into the jump instead of entering it backwards. The skater takes off from the forward outside edge of the left foot and lands backwards on the back outside edge of the right foot. This means that every for a single Axel the the skater rotates 1.5 times. Due to the extra halfway rotation, this is considered as the hardest jump.
